Programmed & Designed by Yasser's Group

Plan

Our plan is to spread awareness about misplacing garbage, to create solutions for this issue, so that both you and I can live in a safer, healthier abode.

Action

We always do our best to become an eco-friendly school, so we created this to keep track of our schools garbage to maintain a healthier enviorment.

Goals

The reason for the VPMS Garbage Audit is so we can track our garbage to keep our hygene flawless, inorder to reach our goal of becoming the best eco-friendly school.

Findings

Our class arranged a garbage audit and this what we discovered:

86% of food waste is not garbage and 14% is recyclable containers and from our results the average student creates 67LB of waste each year Don't buy anything that comes in wasteful single-serving packages, like candy, gum, granola bars, popsicles, etc. Skip buying plastic garbage bags and simply put your trash into the can itself. This will require you to wash the can from time to time, but if you are composting well, it won't get too messy. The average American throws away more than 7 pounds of garbage a day. North america waste 4.5 million tons of paper each year.In the US, Americans toss 60 million water bottles daily, which is nearly 700 each minute, either in the ocean or in the garbage where they don't belong. Have you ever been to a cruise or a buffet ? did you know 7.2 million ton of edible food is thrown away

Facts

Did You Know That. . .

1. Recycling one aluminum roll can save enough energy to run a TV for three hours - or the equivalent of a half a gallon of gasoline.

2. Motor oil never wears out, it just gets dirty. Old oil can be recycled and used again.

3. It takes 80-100 years for an aluminum can to decompose in a landfill.

4. Americans make more than 200 million tons of garbage each year, enough to fill Bush Stadium from top to bottom twice a day!

5. About 50% of the recycling is found in the garbage can and about 40% of the garbage is found in recycling cans.

6. 38 million miles of ribbon are put into landfills each year. That is enough to tie a bow around the earth

7. During the time between Thanksgiving and New Year's Day, an extra million tons of waste is generated--per week.

8. Each ton of paper recycled can save three cubic yards of landfill space

9. An average human throws away around 1,200 pounds of waste each year that could be composted and kept out of the landfill.

10. When Ireland enacted a 15 cent per bag charge, consumers consumed 90 percent fewer bags.

11. Reports show that Americans generate between 250 and 400 million tons of garbage each year--all of which requires landfill space.

12. Americans make up roughly 5% of the world's population, but generate nearly 40% of the world's total waste.

13. If every garbage truck we filled (annually) was placed end-to-end, it would cover half the distance to the moon or ~119,450 miles.

14. In a lifetime, the average human will leave a legacy of 90,000 pounds of trash for future generations.

15. Globally, enough hazardous waste is generated each year to fill the New Orleans Superdome more than 1,500 times over.

16. The only relatively "clean" place on Earth is Antarctica. It is protected by the Antarctic Treaty, which prohibits military activities, mineral mining, nuclear explosions and nuclear waste disposal.

17. Americans throw out roughly 270 million tires annually.

18. Packaging represents about 65% of household trash.

19. Americans throw away 5.7 million tons of carpet every year.

20. Americans throw out roughly 43,000 tons of food daily.

21. In Europe, Africa, and Latin America the food currently wasted could feed nearly 900 million people.

22. Annually, Americans throw out old phones containing over $60 million in gold and/or silver.

23. Recycling one million laptops saves the energy equivalent of powering 3,657 homes.

24. In the United States alone, over 140 million cell phones are thrown into landfills every year.

25. Annually the United States discards roughly 1.6 billion pens and 2 billion razor blades.

Conclusion

In denouement, we need to actually take action about disposing our waste incorrectly, because it will eventually affect all of us. So take the time to make an adjustment to your daily routines, little or great, it all matters and we will all benefit. Think about it, if you make this slight adjustment, is it gonna harm you? Is it a loss? No, it's just a little more effort, so stop procrastinating and act now to live an improved lifestyle and better future.
